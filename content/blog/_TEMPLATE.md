Opening paragraph. State the data point, what moved, and why it matters — in three sentences or fewer. No preamble, no "this week in golf." Lead with the number.

## Section heading (the pattern or context)

Two to three paragraphs of analysis. Connect the data movement to a real-world event: a tournament, a product launch, a seasonal inflection, a weather pattern, a macro trend. Use specific numbers from the dashboard. Compare to prior years.

Format for referencing dashboard data inline: **"golf clubs" hit 91 in August 2025** — bold the key fact. When referencing the dashboard, link to the relevant section: [the annual chart](/) or [the heatmap](/).

## Section heading (the so-what)

What does this mean for someone reading? Retailers timing inventory. Marketers planning content calendars. Industry analysts tracking participation. Give them the actionable takeaway.

Keep it under 500 words. If you're over 500, you're writing two posts — split them.

> End with a blockquote that frames the forward-looking question. What should the reader watch for next? This gives the post a natural close and sets up the next week's brief.
